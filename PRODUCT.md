# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**Operator (Vuthy San):** Developer/admin managing farm clients and asset batches. Not a casual consumer — power and information density are welcome. Desktop browser, daytime, focused work session. One operator runs everything. Accesses the `/dashboard` surface.

**Farmers (Khmer-speaking clients):** Cambodian farmers who receive daily care instructions for their registered batches. Mobile-first (Android smartphones), potentially outdoors in bright conditions, Khmer as primary language, variable tech literacy. Accesses a dedicated `/farm` portal. Farmer portal details to be defined through shape process.

## Product Purpose

VKasekor is an agricultural operations dashboard for managing farm asset batches (chickens, pigs, vegetables/cucumbers) in Cambodia. The operator registers a new batch on day one; the system's Rules Engine automatically calculates daily care instructions and sends Khmer-language Telegram alerts to the farmer. The dashboard is the control surface: register batches, monitor status, track pending alerts, and verify the cron automation is running correctly. Success means zero missed vaccination or feeding windows for active batches.

## Brand Personality

Grounded, authoritative, field-ready. Not a startup tool, not a government portal. The feel is closer to a seasoned agronomist's notebook: organized, earthy, no-nonsense. Tone in copy: direct and factual. No fluff, no encouragement text, no "Let's get started!" energy.

## Anti-references

Consumer agri-apps: no cartoon farm illustrations, oversized emoji, playful color pops, or gamified progress bars. This is a management tool operated by someone who knows what they're doing. Also avoid generic SaaS dashboard templates with blue/purple palettes, gradient-text stats, and hero-metric layouts.

## Design Principles

1. **Density earns its place.** The operator needs fast visual scanning, not breathing-room padding for its own sake. Pack information tightly where it belongs; give air only where hierarchy demands it.
2. **Earthy, not techy.** The green-and-parchment palette should read like the land and working materials, not like a climate-tech startup or an agricultural ministry PDF.
3. **State is the primary signal.** Healthy / At Risk / Harvesting must be instantly readable at a glance — through color, shape, or weight, not just a badge label.
4. **Forms are first-class.** The batch registration form is a core workflow, not an afterthought. It should feel precise and confident, like filling out a field record.
5. **Calm authority.** No animations that perform. No alerts that shout. The system knows what it's doing; the UI reflects that.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the baseline. Internal tool, single operator — no special accommodation constraints identified. Ensure sufficient contrast for status indicators, as colorblindness affects label readability.
